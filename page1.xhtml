1. NIT Ghat(Gandhi Ghat)

Suggested duration :- 2-3 hours

Gandhi Ghat is one of the main Ghats on the Ganges River in Patna. It is named after the leader of the Indian independence movement Mahatma Gandhi. The ghat is famous for its evening Ganga Aarti. It is also associated with the immersion of ashes of Mahatma Gandhi in the river Ganges. It is a nice riverfront located at the bank of river ganga. It is a hangout place for locals especially among NIT students. Locals and students enjoy their eveining. People enjoy sightseeing view of River Ganga. It is a peaceful and calm place full with temples. One can cross river ganga from there by a boat and get the experience of boating as well.

Map :-

Location :-

Gandhi Ghat is situated on the bank of the Ganges. It is located behind National Institute of Technology, Patna and is around 5 Kilometers North East of Patna Junction railway station.

Points of intersets :-

● Ganga Aarti :-


You might wanna visit this place in the evening because the main attraction of NIT Ghat, The Ganga Aarti. The cool breeze with the heartwarming scenery will sooth your mind and soul. Five pujaris, dressed in white dhoti and red kurtas, specially trained in Varanasi, carry out the rituals on the river bank. The light of ‘diyas’ illuminated the riverbank amidst chanting of hymns.Ganga Aarti on Gandhi Ghat is performed with 51 lamps, by a group of priests, dressed in saffron robes. The Aarti starts with the blowing of a conch shell and continues with the movement of incense sticks in elaborate patterns and circling of large burning lamps that create a bright hue against the darkened sky. A huge gathering of devotees, including children and old people, gather by the river side to observe the ‘aarti’. A number of diyas are floated in the river at the end.

● The Floating Restaurant :-


Bihar State Tourism Development Corporation (BSTDC) operates MV Ganga Vihar from Gandhi Ghat. Ganga Vihar is a River Cruise ship with restaurant on-board also known as the floating restaurant. It runs Sunset Cruise and Leisure Cruise/Corporate Cruise with boarding for tourists from the ghat. This particular activity draws a lot of tourists to come and feast at the ‘floating restaurant’ amidst warm blue waters of the Ganges. Another vessel MV Kautilya was added in 2016 which is a cruise boat for tourist rides on river Ganges from Gandhi Ghat. The Tourism Department also plans to keep a floating jetty that would be placed at Gandhi Ghat, from where the two vessels operate.

● The Kite Festival :-


The Kite Festival first started in 2011 and has been organised every year since then. State tourism department organises a kite festival on the Sabbalpur diara island, across the river on the occasion of Makar Sankranti. Honourable Chief Minister himself come here to celebrate the festival. If you wish to celebrate the festival here, you are gonna have to come here on the Makar Sankranti day.
